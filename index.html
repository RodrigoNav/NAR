6G and Non-Terrestrial Networks (NTN)

Are key concepts in the evolution of wireless communication technology.

6G represents the sixth generation of mobile network technology, following 5G. It is anticipated to bring significant advancements over its predecessor, including higher spectral efficiency, improved communication energy efficiency, and increased data transmission rates. 6G aims to provide broader network spectral bandwidth and supports the integration of non-terrestrial networks (NTNs) to overcome the coverage limitations of 5G networks. This support is crucial for ensuring service availability, continuity, ubiquity, and scalability in global, pervasive, and uninterrupted connectivity.


Non-Terrestrial Networks (NTNs), such as satellite networks, play a fundamental role in providing global coverage for applications requiring high availability and resilience. These networks complement terrestrial coverage, especially in rural areas and over vast oceans and deserts, where terrestrial services are not available. Satellite communication is well-suited for serving tracking and maritime applications, providing resilience, addressing low-bandwidth machine type communications, broadcast, and other delay-tolerant services. The 5G standards have been adapted to support Low-Earth Orbit (LEO) and Geosynchronous Orbit (GEO) satellite communications, and the 5G NTN is based on the bent pipe architecture, where a feeder link signal is amplified and changed in frequency when forwarded to the service link.
